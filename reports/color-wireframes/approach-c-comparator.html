Approach C — Similar Colour Comparator

Inline expansion below a warning row in the palette list. Shown for two pairs: blanc/B5200 (ΔE₀₀ 1.8) and 826/827 (ΔE₀₀ 8.2).

The comparator is triggered by clicking the warning row. It expands inline below the row — no modal, no overlay, no navigation away from the palette. The dismiss action hides the comparator for the current session; the warning row itself persists until one of the two threads is removed from the palette.

Pair 1: blanc / B5200 — ΔE₀₀ 1.8 — comparator expanded

These two whites are within the warning threshold (ΔE₀₀ < 3.0). The comparator shows how close they are and helps the user decide whether to keep both. At 1.8, they are barely perceptible even side by side in person.

Pair 2: 826 / 827 — ΔE₀₀ 8.2 — comparator expanded

At ΔE₀₀ 8.2, these two blues do NOT trigger the warning threshold (ΔE₀₀ < 3.0). This pair is shown to demonstrate the comparator can also be used to confirm that two colours ARE sufficiently distinct. In this case the reading is reassuring: the user can be confident these threads will read as different values in the finished work.

The comparator for this pair could be opened from a manual "compare" affordance (right-click on a row, or a future "compare pair" action), not from a warning row (since no warning fires at ΔE₀₀ 8.2). It is shown here to illustrate how the delta-value description changes with the reading.

Design notes

Tall swatch shape (40×60px). The aspect ratio is deliberately taller than wide, to better simulate how thread lies in a skein or on a needle. A 40×40px square swatch gives the same area but less of the perceptual "thread" impression. The tall shape also makes the colour reading more accurate because it gives more area to absorb before judging the hue.

Delta in the centre, not above. Placing ΔE₀₀ between the two swatches creates an instinctive comparison gesture — the eye moves left swatch, reads the number, right swatch, forms a judgement. Placing it above both would break this flow.

826/827 button reads "Close comparison" not "Dismiss warning." Because no warning was triggered for 826/827, there is no warning to dismiss. The label adapts. In production, a boolean prop isWarning controls the label.

Warning threshold at ΔE₀₀ 3.0. Below 3.0, two colours are genuinely difficult to distinguish in stitched work. At 1.8 (blanc/B5200) most stitchers working under artificial light will not see a difference. At 2.5 the difference becomes marginally perceptible under direct sunlight. At 3.0 it becomes perceptible side-by-side on the fabric. The warning fires at <3.0 not at <2.0 to catch the "close but not quite same" cases that will frustrate users when they realise after stitching.

Dismiss vs remove. "Dismiss warning" hides the comparator for the session. It does NOT remove either thread from the palette — that is a separate action the user must take deliberately. The warning is advisory, not prescriptive.
