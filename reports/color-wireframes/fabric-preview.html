Fabric Background Preview — UI Wireframe

Showing how the fabric background control would appear in the toolbar and its effect on the pattern canvas.

The fabric colour affects how thread colours are perceived, especially for light/pastel threads. Many stitchers work on natural linen or coloured fabric. This control lets them see their pattern in the context of their actual fabric choice.

Toolbar with Fabric Background selector

Fabric:

The selector sits in the existing tool strip, alongside zoom and grid controls. Selecting a fabric updates the canvas background immediately. The selected preference is saved per project.

Canvas comparison across three fabric colours

Same pattern (a red heart motif with white highlight using DMC 321 and B5200), shown on three different fabric backgrounds. Note how the winter white (B5200) cells become nearly invisible on white fabric but read clearly on natural linen.

White Aida (#FFFFFF)

Note: The winter white highlight (B5200, rgb 255,255,255) is invisible against white Aida — stitchers need fabric preview to see this.

Natural Linen (#D2B48C)

On natural linen, the red reads warmer and the white highlight (B5200) is now clearly visible as a distinct, cool white against the warm background.

Black Aida (#1A1A1A)

On black Aida, the red pops dramatically. The white highlight reads as a sharp cool accent. A pattern designed with white background in mind may look completely different on black.

Implementation notes

  • The fabric colour only affects the background cells (cells with id: "__empty__" or id: "__skip__"). Stitched cells are unaffected.
  • Default remains white (#FFFFFF) — no change for existing users unless they choose a different fabric.
  • Preference is stored per project in the project settings object.
  • The canvas renderer (creator/canvasRenderer.js) and the stitch tracker canvas (tracker-app.js) both need updating.
  • The fabric selector is a minimal addition to the existing tool strip — it does not require a new panel or modal.
Colours shown are screen approximations. Thread codes are the authoritative reference.
