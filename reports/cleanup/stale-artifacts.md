## Top 3 summary

1. **Move ~165 historical reports under `reports/_archive/`.** The May-2026 perf-cleanup audit (`00_PERFORMANCE_CONTEXT.md`) already classified `reports/` as a 257-file Phase-2 priority. Most files document completed work (perf cat A, stats fix list, sync phase 4, branch-audit defects 001–015, preview reactivity, import phase 5) and survive only as historical reference. Grouping them under `_archive/` cuts the working `reports/` tree by ~65% without losing git history.
2. **Top-level [SYNC_TEST_PLAN.md](../../SYNC_TEST_PLAN.md) duplicates [reports/sync-9-test-plan.md](../sync-9-test-plan.md)** and [reports/branch-audit/MANUAL_TEST_PLAN.md](../branch-audit/MANUAL_TEST_PLAN.md) is structurally similar. Either fold both report-side files into `SYNC_TEST_PLAN.md` (keep the top-level QA doc; archive the reports) or vice versa. Today both exist, both are referenced, and updates only land in one.
3. **`reports/perf-results/{import,interactions,startup}.json` are raw benchmark output** committed to source. They should either be moved out of `reports/` (they aren't human-readable docs), regenerated on demand by `npm run perf:baseline`, or gitignored. The `.gitignore` already excludes `tests/.tmp/`; same treatment fits here.
