Confetti cleanup
Removes isolated single stitches that are fiddly to stitch. "Moderate" is recommended for most photos.

Confetti stitches are single stitches of one colour surrounded entirely by different colours. Each one forces you to stop, knot off the thread, and start a new colour — which can triple your stitching time in busy areas.

Stitch Score at each level

~42 / 100

Higher levels remove more stitches, which can soften fine details in photos. Check the preview before committing.

Dithering
How the app fills in colours between your palette. Floyd-Steinberg gives smooth gradients — leave at default unless you want a flat, graphic look.

Dithering approximates colours that aren't in your palette by mixing nearby colours in a pattern.

Floyd-Steinberg: Distributes the "error" between actual and nearest-palette colour to neighbouring stitches. Results in smooth-looking gradients. Best for most photos.

Ordered (Bayer): Uses a regular grid pattern. Creates a more visible texture but can look more "digital" and graphic. Good for geometric or low-colour designs.

None: Each stitch takes the nearest palette colour with no mixing. Fastest to compute; can produce visible colour banding.

Orphan removal
Removes completely surrounded single stitches during generation (before the stitch score is calculated). Level 0 = off, level 4 = most aggressive.

Unlike Confetti Cleanup (which is post-processing), orphan removal runs during the colour quantisation step. This means it can affect the palette itself — colours used only in isolated stitches may be removed entirely from the pattern.

Use this if you want to reduce your palette size while also cleaning up stray stitches in one pass. For most users, Confetti Cleanup after generation gives more predictable results.
