Understanding fabric count

Generating patterns · 3 min read

Fabric count (also called "count" or "Aida count") refers to the number of holes per inch in your embroidery fabric. It is one of the most important settings when generating a pattern.

How it affects your finished size

A pattern that is 100 stitches wide will have a different physical size depending on your fabric count:

  • 11 count: ~23 cm (9 inches)
  • 14 count: ~18 cm (7 inches) — most common for beginners
  • 18 count: ~14 cm (5.5 inches) — finer detail, smaller stitches
  • 28 count evenweave: ~9 cm (3.5 inches) stitched over two
Tip
If you already have fabric, measure it in inches and count the holes across one inch to find your count. Most fabric sold in hobby shops is 14ct Aida.

How it affects thread requirements

Higher fabric counts require more passes per inch, increasing the thread density and the number of skeins you will need. The Materials tab will recalculate automatically when you change the count.
